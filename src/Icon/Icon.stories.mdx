# Icon

### Bulma is compatible with **all icon font libraries**

[Bulma Documentation](https://bulma.io/documentation/elements/icon/)

Supported Versions: `0.9.3`, `0.9.2`

## Default

The `<Icon>` component is a **container** for any type of **icon font**. Because the icons can take a few seconds to load, and because you want control over the **space** the icons will take, you can use the `Icon` component as a reliable square container that will prevent the page to "jump" on page load.

These examples use [React Fontawesome](https://github.com/FortAwesome/react-fontawesome) with Font Awesome icons.

<Canvas>
  <Story name="Default">
    <Icon>
      <FontAwesomeIcon icon={faHome} />
    </Icon>
  </Story>
</Canvas>

By default, the `<Icon>` container will take up exactly `1.5rem x 1.5rem`. The icon itself is sized accordingly to the icon library you're using. For example, Font Awesome 5 icons will **inherit** the font size.

## Icon Text

You can combine an icon with **text**, using the `<Icon.Text>` wrapper, as long as all text inside is wrapped in its own `<span>` element:

<Canvas>
  <Story name="Icon Text">
    <Icon.Text>
      <Icon>
        <FontAwesomeIcon icon={faHome} />
      </Icon>
      <span>Home</span>
    </Icon.Text>
  </Story>
</Canvas>

You can combine **as many** `<Icon>` elements and text elements as you want:

<Canvas>
  <Story name="Icon Text Multiple">
    <Icon.Text>
      <Icon>
        <FontAwesomeIcon icon={faTrain} />
      </Icon>
      <span>Paris</span>
      <Icon>
        <FontAwesomeIcon icon={faArrowRight} />
      </Icon>
      <span>Budapest</span>
      <Icon>
        <FontAwesomeIcon icon={faArrowRight} />
      </Icon>
      <span>Bucharest</span>
      <Icon>
        <FontAwesomeIcon icon={faArrowRight} />
      </Icon>
      <span>Istanbul</span>
      <Icon>
        <FontAwesomeIcon icon={faFlagCheckered} />
      </Icon>
    </Icon.Text>
  </Story>
</Canvas>

Since `<Icon.Text>` is an `inline-flex` element, it can easily be inserted within any paragraph of **text**.

<Canvas>
  <Story name="Icon Text Paragraph">
    <Content>
      <p>
        An invitation to
        <Icon.Text>
          <Icon>
            <FontAwesomeIcon icon={faUtensils} />
          </Icon>
          <span>dinner</span>
        </Icon.Text>
        was soon afterwards dispatched; and already had Mrs. Bennet planned the courses that were to do credit to her housekeeping, when an answer arrived which deferred it all. Mr. Bingley was obliged to be in
        <Icon.Text>
          <Icon>
            <FontAwesomeIcon icon={faCity} />
          </Icon>
          <span>town</span>
        </Icon.Text>
        the following day, and, consequently, unable to accept the honour of their
        <Icon.Text>
          <Icon>
            <FontAwesomeIcon icon={faEnvelopeOpenText} />
          </Icon>
          <span>invitation</span>
        </Icon.Text>,
        etc.
      </p>
      <p>
        Mrs. Bennet was quite disconcerted. She could not imagine what business he could have in town so soon after his
        <Icon.Text>
          <Icon>
            <FontAwesomeIcon icon={faFlagCheckered} />
          </Icon>
          <span>arrival</span>
        </Icon.Text>
        in Hertfordshire; and she began to fear that he might be always
        <Icon.Text>
          <Icon>
            <FontAwesomeIcon icon={faPlaneDeparture} />
          </Icon>
          <span>flying</span>
        </Icon.Text>
        about from one place to another, and never settled at Netherfield as he ought to be.
      </p>
    </Content>
  </Story>
</Canvas>

You can also turn the `<Icon.Text>` into a `flex` element simply by rendering it as a `div` component.

<Canvas>
  <Story name="Icon Text Flex">
    <React.Fragment>
      <Icon.Text component="div">
        <Icon color="info">
          <FontAwesomeIcon icon={faInfoCircle} />
        </Icon>
        <span>Information</span>
      </Icon.Text>
      <Block>
        Your package will be delivered on <strong>Tuesday at 08:00</strong>.
      </Block>
      <Icon.Text component="div">
        <Icon color="success">
          <FontAwesomeIcon icon={faCheckSquare} />
        </Icon>
        <span>Success</span>
      </Icon.Text>
      <Block>
        Your image has been successfully uploaded.
      </Block>
      <Icon.Text component="div">
        <Icon color="warning">
          <FontAwesomeIcon icon={faExclamationTriangle} />
        </Icon>
        <span>Warning</span>
      </Icon.Text>
      <Block>
        Some information is missing from your <a href="#">profile</a> details.
      </Block>
      <Icon.Text component="div">
        <Icon color="danger">
          <FontAwesomeIcon icon={faBan} />
        </Icon>
        <span>Danger</span>
      </Icon.Text>
      <Block>
        There was an error in your submission. <a href="#">Please try again</a>.
      </Block>
    </React.Fragment>
  </Story>
</Canvas>

## Sizes

The `<Icon>` container comes in 4 sizes by use of the `size` prop. Reference the [Bulma Documentation](https://bulma.io/documentation/elements/icon/#sizes) to see how they work with different icon sizes. The background

The background is only here for demonstration purposes, to highlight the icon container's area.

<Canvas>
  <Story name="Sizes">
    <React.Fragment>
      <Block>
        <Icon className="has-background-warning" size="small">
          <FontAwesomeIcon icon={faHome} />
        </Icon>
      </Block>
      <Block>
        <Icon className="has-background-warning" >
          <FontAwesomeIcon icon={faHome} />
        </Icon>

  </Story>
</Canvas>
